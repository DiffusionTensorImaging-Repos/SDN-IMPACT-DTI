IMPACT · Memory data & d′

How the memory scores are computed, cleaned, and what they show — for the 53–54 mothers in the tract analyses.  ·  ← Results Explorer

1 · How d′ is computed

Standard signal-detection theory, computed directly from each mother's raw trial files.

At encoding, a mother chooses between two faces (social) or two doors (monetary). At recall she sees items and says whether she "remembers" each. "Old" items = the ones she chose at encoding; "new" items = the foils she saw but did not choose.

hit rate (H) = "remember" responses to OLD items / # OLD items false-alarm (F) = "remember" responses to NEW items / # NEW items d′ = z(H) − z(F) # z = inverse-normal (probit)
We compute d′ directly from the raw trial files with this standard formula (not from a pre-scored export). As a cross-check it matches the study's separately-exported d′ — monetary near-perfectly, social strongly:

2 · Data cleaning

Only genuinely broken sessions were removed — the formula was never changed.

Two recall sessions were unusable and were excluded on data-quality grounds:

SubjectProblemEffect if kept
s1694Only 16% of "recalled" items were ever shown at encoding — a corrupted / mismatched sessiond′ ≈ −1.0 (nonsense)
s1350Missed > ⅓ of recall trialsUnreliable, near-chance d′
These two sessions produce garbage d′ that was dragging the group social mean to zero. With them removed, social d′ is a genuine above-chance signal (Section 3). No other subject was excluded for d′. Two additional mothers drop from the bias metrics only (s4127, s4210): across the entire social recall they never once judged a face as a "loss" (negative) outcome — every response was "win" or "neutral." Positivity bias is a positive-minus-negative contrast, so with zero negative responses it is mathematically undefined for them (not missing data — they are maximal-positivity responders). d′ is unaffected and keeps them.

4 · Why is social d′ lower than monetary? — the breakdown

d′ has two ingredients: hit rate (memory strength) and false-alarm rate (false positives). Splitting them shows the difference is entirely in false alarms.

Hit rate (remembered correctly)0.5680.548+0.021t=0.86, p=0.395no difference

Social and monetary items are remembered equally well — hit rates are essentially identical (0.568 vs 0.548, p = 0.395). The only difference is that the social task produces significantly more false alarms (0.536 vs 0.47, p = 0.0079): mothers more often falsely "remember" social faces they never chose. So the lower social d′ is not weaker memory — it's more false positives.
This is exactly the phenomenon the tract findings are about. The robust VTA→HPC effect is on social positivity bias in false memories — and here we see, at the behavioural level, that false memories are precisely what distinguishes the social task. Memory strength is matched across domains; the social signal lives in the false-alarm side.
